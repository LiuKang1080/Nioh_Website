Combat Mechanics


Nioh offers an extremely deep, but complex combat system. There are 6 major sections that make up the flow of combat in Nioh: Light Attacks, Heavy Attacks, Special Attacks, Ki Pulse, Stance Switching, and Flux. By learning and utilizing these mechanics during combat one can move and attack extreamly fast without running out of Ki. TODO: "I'm a SoulsBorne Vet!..." Nioh isn't like a souls game; it's more like Ninja Gaiden, Devil May Cry, or Bayonetta...


Light Attacks:

(Square / X) Light attacks are a staple in your combat arsenal. You will be using them very frequently to deal damage very fast. Many light attacks are used as combo starters leading into heavy attacks or special attacks. Light attacks consume less Ki compared to heavy attacks and certain special attacks.


Heavy Attacks:

(Triangle / Y) Heavy attacks are the second form of basic attacks. Generally these attacks are slower than light attacks and consume more Ki. Certain heavy attacks are used as combo enders, along with special attacks. It is very important to learn both light and heavy attacks for each stance: Low, Mid, and High Stance.


Ki Pulse:

(R1 / RB) The Ki pulse mechanic is the most important mechanic in Nioh. It allows one to re-gain Ki faster, and is tied together with the Stance system. After executing light, heavy, or special attacks the Ki bar will start to regenerate with a white bar from where the current Ki is. The white bar shows how much Ki will be recovered if the user performs a Ki Pulse.

The user can perform a Ki Pulse by pressing R1 / RB at any time the white bar is active. By performing a Ki Pulse when the white bar is at the maximum, the user will perform a Perfect Ki Pulse.

When these Yokai spawn in, they will always leave a Yokai pool. Yokai pools slow the regeneration of Ki when you stand in them. Yokai pools can be purified by performing a perfect Ki Pulse. It must be a perfect Ki Pulse, a normal non-perfect Ki Pulse will not purify the Yokai pool. Certain skills can be unlocked that grant bonuses to the player when they purify Yokai pools with perfect Ki Pulses. Performing Flux I and II when the white bar is at the maximum will also be counted as performing a perfect Ki Pulse. If the skills Living Water Heaven, Living Water Man, and Living Water Earth are unlocked, the user can perform a perfect Ki Pulse by dodging at the proper time.

It is extremely important to develop the habit of performing Ki Pulses! One should aim to perform a Ki Pulse after every combo and special attack to build proper habits.

Side Note: Yokai pools can also be purified by: Throwing Salt at the Yokai pool, using the Kekkai Talisman, and Shooting Hamaya Arrows at the Yokai pool. While in Living Weapon, pressing R1 / RB after combos will also Ki Pulse and purify Yokai Pools.
